What is ASTRO?

ASTRO stands for the Air Spora Trapping and Recovery Operation. The goal of our project is to create a spore collection device that will allow us to explore the preservation of genomic integrity of biotic particles residing at high altitudes. Once the device is complete, we plan to fly it as a payload on the NASA High Altitude Student Platform.


We are driven by a biological query: Given that organisms evolve in order to adapt to changing conditions, how would the extreme environment of high altitudes affect genetic variation? This project will allow us the opportunity to address this question, as well as further our understanding of evolutionary adaptations on the purely genetic level.

Why are we doing this?

In addition to the academic pursuit of knowledge, this project also provides students with a variety of opportunities. Students are able to enhance their understanding of or technical skills in the areas of design, prototyping, and electronics. The students will be able to interface with other university teams and members of the NASA community, providing valuable networking opportunities. Also, the ASTRO team creates a precedent for MIT's participation on the HASP Balloon project, which could allow for easier acceptance for other student projects in the future.
